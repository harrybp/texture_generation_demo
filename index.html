Texture Synthesis with Convolutional Neural Networks

Final Year Project by Harry Bond-Preston

Comparing two approaches to texture generation

Optimising on random noise as proposed by gatys et al. A single pretrained discriminator can be used to generate from any source image. Here the demonstration shows the real time noise image as it is generated over 300 iterations.

Generational Adversarial Networks or 'GANS'. Here the generators are pretrained for 140k iterations on random crops of the source image. A checkpoint of the generator is saved every 500 iterations, the demonstration generates an image using each checkpoint to show the learning process.
